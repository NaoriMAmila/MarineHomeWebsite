About Marine Life

Marine life includes all organisms that live in the oceans, from the smallest plankton to the largest whales. Our oceans are home to millions of species that contribute to the health of the planet and provide vital resources for humans.

Species

Marine ecosystems support a diverse range of species, each playing a crucial role in maintaining ecological balance. Here are a few examples:

Sea Turtles

Graceful creatures that have existed for millions of years, helping to maintain healthy seagrass beds.

Whales

The gentle giants of the ocean, whales play a key role in the marine food chain and carbon cycling.

Corals

Vital for marine biodiversity, coral reefs provide habitats for countless marine organisms.

Conservation Efforts

Protecting marine life is essential for sustaining biodiversity and human life. Conservation initiatives focus on reducing pollution, combating climate change, and preserving habitats.
